Qianlong Emperor (1711–1799)

Qianlong Emperor was one of the longest-reigning emperors in Chinese history, ruling the Qing Dynasty from 1735 to 1796. His reign marked the peak of Qing power, territorial expansion, and cultural flourishing.

Born as Hongli, he was well-educated in literature, calligraphy, and martial arts. His rule expanded China’s borders, strengthened governance, and saw remarkable artistic achievements.

1735 - Ascension

Qianlong became emperor following the death of his father, the Yongzheng Emperor.

1755-1760 - Xinjiang Campaign

His military campaigns expanded China's borders, bringing Xinjiang under Qing control.

1796 - Abdication

After 60 years, he abdicated in favor of his son, Jiaqing Emperor, but retained influence.

Legacy

Qianlong's reign left a lasting impact on China’s culture, art, and governance. His collection of artworks and literary projects preserved Chinese heritage for generations.
