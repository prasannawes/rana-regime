Timeline

1711 - Born as Hongli.

1735 - Became Emperor of the Qing Dynasty.

1755-1760 - Conquered Xinjiang, expanding Qing control.

1770s - Commissioned the Siku Quanshu, the largest collection of Chinese literature.

1796 - Abdicated in favor of his son, but remained influential.

1799 - Passed away at the age of 87.

Legacy

Qianlong’s reign was a golden age for Chinese art and culture. He was a patron of the arts, calligraphy, and poetry, personally writing over 40,000 poems. He commissioned the restoration of historical sites, including the Forbidden City and the Summer Palace.

Despite his successes, his later years were criticized for corruption and extravagance, leading to the decline of the Qing Dynasty. Nevertheless, his military conquests and cultural contributions shaped China’s history.
