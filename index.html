The Rana Regime (1846–1951)

The Rana Regime was a period of autocratic rule in Nepal that lasted from 1846 to 1951. The Rana dynasty came to power after the Kot Massacre, in which Jung Bahadur Rana eliminated his rivals and took control as the Prime Minister.

The Ranas established a feudal system, suppressing democracy and limiting education. Nepal remained isolated while maintaining ties with British India.

The revolution of 1951, supported by democratic forces and King Tribhuvan, led to the downfall of the Rana rule, marking a major shift in Nepalese governance.

1846 - Kot Massacre

The massacre eliminated opposition and established Jung Bahadur Rana’s rule.

1856 - Nepal-British Treaty

The Ranas strengthened their relationship with British India.

1934 - Limited Modernization

Infrastructure development was initiated but remained limited to elites.

1951 - Revolution

The democratic movement and King Tribhuvan overthrew the Rana regime.
